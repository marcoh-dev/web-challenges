About

Steve Carter is a marine biologist who has spent most of his life fascinated by the mysteries of the ocean. Growing up near a rugged coastline, he developed an early curiosity about sea creatures, tide pools, and the rhythms of the tides. That childhood curiosity eventually turned into a career dedicated to studying marine ecosystems and protecting ocean life.

Today, Steve works for a coastal research institute where he studies coral reef health and the behavior of migratory fish. His days are a mix of fieldwork and research—sometimes diving beneath the waves to collect samples, and other times analyzing data in the lab to better understand how changing ocean conditions affect marine species.

Known among his colleagues for his calm attitude and endless curiosity, Steve believes that science is not just about discovery but also about responsibility. He regularly gives talks at local schools and community events, hoping to inspire others to care about the oceans as much as he does.

When he isn’t working, Steve enjoys sailing, underwater photography, and documenting the fascinating creatures he encounters during his dives. For him, every day in the ocean is a reminder of how much there is still left to learn—and protect. 🌊🐠

“Ever since I first looked beneath the surface of the ocean, I’ve felt a responsibility to understand and protect it. The sea isn’t just where I work—it’s where my curiosity lives. Every dive reminds me that the more we learn about our oceans, the better chance we have of preserving their incredible life for future generations.”

Experience

Steve has worked for the following partners:

  • Ocean Conservancy – 2 years
    Steve partnered with this organization on a project studying how plastic pollution affects coral reef ecosystems.
  • National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration – 3 years
    He worked with NOAA researchers to track migratory fish patterns and analyze ocean temperature changes.
  • World Wildlife Fund – 1 year
    Steve assisted in a conservation program focused on protecting endangered marine species and reef habitats. 🌊🐠
